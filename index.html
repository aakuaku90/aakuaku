About Me

My journey began with a deep love for Actuarial Science, where I gained expertise in analyzing risks and solving complex mathematical problems in insurance and finance. During this period, I researched the "herding effect" of investors in the forex market, igniting my passion for understanding human behavior and decision-making.

This led me to Behavioral Science and Information Systems, fields that allow me to apply my Actuarial skills to address the world's most wicked challenges. I moved to Berkeley to expand my knowledge in Information Systems and tackle issues like the social dilemmas created by technology. Today, I focus on studying human behavioral patterns in food, AI, and sleep, working to develop humane solutions that reduce risks and create a better, more balanced world. Happy to chat!!
