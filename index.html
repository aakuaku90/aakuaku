About Me

I pursued a degree in Actuarial Science, where I developed expertise in analyzing risks and solving complex mathematical problems within the realms of insurance and finance. During this time, I conducted research on the “herding effect” of investors in the forex market, which sparked my deep interest in understanding human behavior and decision-making processes.

This passion guided me toward the fields of Behavioral Science and Information Systems, where I could apply my actuarial expertise to address some of the world’s most complex challenges. To further this pursuit, I moved to Berkeley to expand my knowledge of Information Systems and work on issues such as the social dilemmas posed by technology. Currently, my research focuses on human behavioral patterns in food, artificial intelligence, and sleep, with a commitment to developing humane solutions that mitigate risks and promote a more balanced and sustainable world.
